# Ready-To-Film Scripts

Every post uses a vertical face-camera and Mac screen-recording mix. Put `Mac
app` in the first overlay and use the final CTA card for the last 1.5 seconds.
Captions intentionally stay concise and always end with `hotblock.app`.

## Week 1: Establish The Premise

### HB01 — I Built An App That Gets Annoyed

**Hook overlay:** `I built a Mac app that gets annoyed when I procrastinate`

**Shots:** Start on the browser opening TikTok. Let Hotblock close the tab and
say, “No. Not TikTok right now.” Cut immediately to a mildly offended face
reaction.

**Line:** `It has unfortunately started working.`

**Caption:** `Built a focus app. Accidentally gave it authority. Free for Mac at hotblock.app`

### HB02 — Deadline In Two Hours

**Hook overlay:** `POV: your deadline is in two hours`

**Shots:** Show the deadline or unfinished document for half a second. Open
YouTube. Let Hotblock close it and say, “Seriously? You opened YouTube again.”
Hold a silent stare at the screen.

**Line:** `I made this thing and somehow I'm still the victim.`

**Caption:** `My own Mac app has chosen sides. Open hotblock.app on your Mac`

### HB03 — Every Attempt Makes It Angrier

**Hook overlay:** `Every time I procrastinate, it gets more annoyed`

**Shots:** Rapidly attempt Instagram, Reddit, then YouTube. Keep each tab close
and voice response. End on the strongest face reaction.

**Line:** `The third one felt personal.`

**Caption:** `Should I make the next version meaner? hotblock.app`

### HB04 — Why Normal Blockers Fail Me

**Hook line:** `Normal website blockers make one dangerous assumption.`

**Shots:** Talk directly to camera, then show a normal-looking off button
followed by Hotblock strict mode closing a distracting tab.

**Line:** `That I can be trusted with an off button. So I built one that argues back.`

**Caption:** `A strict Mac blocker for people who negotiate with themselves. hotblock.app`

### HB05 — How Strict Should A Blocker Be?

**Hook overlay:** `How strict should a Mac focus app be?`

**Shots:** Move the unlock-word slider from a small value to 300. Attempt to
stop strict mode and reveal the challenge. Cut to a slow face reaction.

**Line:** `This may have been an overcorrection.`

**Caption:** `One to 300 random words. Choose responsibly. hotblock.app`

## Week 4: Prove Value And Convert

### HB16 — Before And After Hotblock

**Hook overlay:** `Working without vs. with my strict Mac blocker`

**Shots:** Use a split structure. Before: repeatedly switching to distracting
sites. After: Hotblock closes one attempt and the work continues. Keep the
comparison honest and modest.

**Line:** `Still distractible. Now inconvenienced in the correct direction.`

**Caption:** `Open hotblock.app on your Mac`

### HB17 — Three Weeks Building Hotblock

**Hook line:** `Three weeks ago, almost nobody knew this app existed.`

**Shots:** Face camera with real metrics on screen: posts, comments, downloads,
and the most requested improvement. Hide any private dashboard information.

**Line:** `Here is what people actually cared about, and what I'm fixing next.`

**Caption:** `Building a strict Mac blocker in public. hotblock.app`

### HB18 — The Winner Recut

**Hook overlay:** Use a new first sentence on the sprint's best-performing
concept. Examples: `My Mac now calls me out` or `This is what happens when I open YouTube`.

**Shots:** Recreate the winner with a different blocked site and a tighter first
two seconds. Preserve the original payoff structure.

**Line:** Use a new dry reaction line rather than repeating the original.

**Caption:** `The experiment continues. hotblock.app`

### HB19 — One Week Using My Own App

**Hook overlay:** `What actually changed after one week using Hotblock`

**Shots:** Face camera with honest observations and one real product demo.
Mention one success and one limitation. Do not promise universal outcomes.

**Line:** `It did not make me disciplined. It made procrastinating much more embarrassing.`

**Caption:** `Free strict website blocker for Mac: hotblock.app`
